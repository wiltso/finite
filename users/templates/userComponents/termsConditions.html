Terms and conditions

By using Finite service and creating a Finite account, you agree to allow everyone using this service to access your schedule. Everyone, who has an account, can see your username and all schedule information available from the submitted URL. After you have submitted a Wilma URL, all your friends and the head of development (finite.fi(at)gmail.com), will be able to see your username as well as the classes you are on and all of the information listed above. In your settings, you can turn on and off “private mode”(default turned on), which limits the users that can see your schedule information to friends you have accepted in the service.

Privacy

The most of the data this service collects comes form the wilma link. The other data we collect is how often you use this service. This means that if you are logged in we will save that you have logged in. We also save the last time you accessed the site when you are logged in. The data is stored in a database that only our head developer has access to. our head developer is the only one who can access the data and dosen't share it with any one. There is an inbuilt logger that logs of all of the connections made to the server.

Wilma link submission

When you submit the wilma link our head developer will get notifyed per mail how the submission whent. This means that your class, school and user id will be sent per mail. The mail also contains all of the titles of the classes you are on

Data handling

With an increasing user base data protection is coming a bigger part of this service. Nowadays all personal data that is collected from the submission of the wilma url will be encrypted or hashed. The encryption process uses a hashed password that has salt added to it and encrypts the data with advanced encryption standards.

Cookies

This site uses two cookies for security. One of the cookies is a CSRF cookie that provides security for the user's personal information. The second one is for authenticated users so that the server can verify that a person actually is them.
